Pain relief plan - ACL knee reconstruction

Days 0-14 (first 2 weeks) Set by Dr Ed Halvey Display only - not personalised

Most patients recover well with a simple layered approach. Take the regular medicines on schedule; only step up to the stronger medicine if you genuinely need it.

1

Regular medicines

Take paracetamol (Panadol Osteo) and your anti-inflammatory regularly. These do most of the work.

2

Check your pain

Still stopping you moving or sleeping, or 7/10 or higher? Wait 30-60 min after your regular medicines before deciding.

3

Stronger medicine, only if needed

Tapentadol is a strong opioid baseline. Many patients need little or none. Use for the shortest time possible.

Important safety information

💧 If you become dehydrated, stop the anti-inflammatory

If you are vomiting, have diarrhoea or are not drinking enough, stop the celecoxib / etoricoxib until you are well and drinking normally again.

⏱️ Stop the strong opioid by 2 weeks

You should no longer be taking sustained / slow-release tapentadol beyond 3 weeks - ideally by 2 weeks. Use it for the shortest time you can.

🔒 This is your personal plan

This plan was prescribed for you and your operation. Do not share your medicines or this plan with anyone else.

💊 Don't double up

Only take what is listed, at the times listed. Never exceed the maximum dose written on your prescription label. If unsure, contact the rooms before taking more.
